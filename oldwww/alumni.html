It's in fact due to the continuous support of our Alumni that many activities have taken place over the years, and so we dedicate this space to all our previous coaches and players.


Previous Coaches

Jack Barry

[By the MIT Department of Athletics, 1996]
No one currently working at MIT has been employed by the Department of Athletics longer than Jack Barry. Jack came to MIT in 1959, and even following his "retirement" in 1987, Jack continues as golf coach. During his coaching tenure, Jack has guided Engineer teams into battle over 800 times. His golf teams have registered a 263-135-4 (.659) record through 22 seasons. He has the most wins of any basketball coach in the history of the Institute (162-122, .570), and put together eight consecutive winning seasons from 1961-1968. He was the New England College Division Coach of the Year in 1961-62 when his teams won a school record 15 straight games, and his 1966-67 squad was Eastern College Athletic Conference Team of the Year. Jack also spent eight years as varsity baseball coach compiling a 44-99-2 ledger. Jack increased MIT's visibility and reputation on an international scale by venturing into competition with both Canadian and European teams. A 1941 University of Michigan graduate, Jack spent from 1968-1987 as assistant athletic director in addition to his coaching responsibilities.

Coach Barry retired at the end of the Fall Season of 1996.
